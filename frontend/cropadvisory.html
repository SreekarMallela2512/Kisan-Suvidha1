Crop Selection Guide

Choosing the right crop depends on soil type, climate, and market demand. Key factors:

  • Clay soil: Wheat, barley (retains moisture)
  • Sandy soil: Peanuts, carrots (good drainage)
  • pH 6-7: Most vegetables thrive

Rotation Tip: Alternate legumes (soybean) with cereals (wheat) to restore nitrogen.

Soil Health Management

Healthy soil contains:

  • 45% minerals (sand, silt, clay)
  • 5% organic matter (compost, manure)
  • 25% water and 25% air

Test soil every 2 years for N-P-K levels and pH balance.

Quick Fix: Add lime if pH < 6, sulfur if pH > 7.5.

Planting Techniques

Seed Depth Rule: Plant at 2-3x the seed's diameter.

  • Direct seeding: Beans, corn (plant after last frost)
  • Transplanting: Tomatoes, peppers (start indoors)

Spacing matters: Crowding reduces yield by 20-30%.

Pest Management

Integrated Pest Management (IPM):

  • Prevention: Resistant varieties, crop rotation
  • Monitoring: Scout weekly for pests
  • Control: Neem oil (organic), pyrethrins (last resort)

Common pests: Aphids (suck sap), Cutworms (stem damage).

Harvesting Tips

Harvest indicators:

  • Leafy greens: Before flowering
  • Tomatoes: Firm, full color
  • Grains: When moisture < 20%

Morning harvest retains more moisture. Use clean tools to prevent disease.

Fertilizer Guide

N-P-K Basics:

  • Nitrogen (N): Leaf growth (apply early)
  • Phosphorus (P): Root development
  • Potassium (K): Disease resistance

Organic options: Compost (1-1-1), Bone meal (0-10-0).
